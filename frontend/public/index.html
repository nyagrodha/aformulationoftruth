गा व्य-स्या त्म-सं सा रे क वी रे व प्र जा प ति: य था स् मै रो च ते वि ष्वं तथे दं प रि व र्त ते
In the self-world of poetry, the poet alone is the Prajāpati, the lord of creation.
Just as it pleases him, so too does the world transform.
truth resides in the reconstruction of events without precedent
in a world where nothing repeats itself exactly; where sense
comes from the association of memory.
to be a person, to act as one ought,
to act according to the categorical imperative,
in other words, to be oneself cohesive through time (having said something thoughtless)
one may not want to identify a 'self' as unified in any 'person'
