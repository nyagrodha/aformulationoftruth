a slow experiment

In my wanderings through the corridors of memory, I discovered that time, like the sacred rivers of India, flows not in straight lines but in endless spirals. The madeleine that unlocked my childhood was no different from the sacred प्रसाद that dissolves on the tongue, releasing floods of divine remembrance.

நேரம் என்பது ஒரு சக்கரம், திரும்பும் பாதை

भावनाओं का जाल

"The real voyage of discovery consists not in seeking new landscapes, but in having new eyes—eyes that have gazed upon the infinite forms of the divine, from the temples of Tamil Nadu to the ghats of Varanasi."

Each emotion, I learned, is like a raga—a musical mode that colors the entire landscape of experience. Love, jealousy, desire: they are not mere feelings but cosmic principles, देवताs that govern the theater of consciousness.

Maya and Memory

What the Vedantins call माया, I recognized in the perpetual dance between memory and reality. The past is neither lost nor preserved—it exists in a realm beyond time, accessible only through the sacred act of remembrance, like the temple dancer who embodies the eternal through the temporal.

மாயை என்பது நினைவின் அழகிய பிரம்மை
माया स्मृतिर्ब्रह्म

The Eternal Return

In the cyclical philosophy of India, I found the key to understanding why certain moments return to us with such vivid intensity. They are not mere memories but eternal forms—प्लातिक ideals that manifest repeatedly across the wheel of time.

"Time regained is not time remembered, but time recognized as having never been lost."

सत्चितानंद

The trinity of existence-consciousness-bliss that forms the foundation of Vedantic thought mirrors the structure of artistic creation. In writing, we seek not merely to exist (सत्), but to become conscious (चित्) of existence, and through that consciousness, to touch the bliss (आनंद) of pure being.

உண்மை, அறிவு, ஆனந்தம் - கலையின் முத்தொகுதி

काव्य और कर्म

In the Bhagavad Gita's teaching of निष्काम कर्म, I found the secret of artistic creation. To write without attachment to result, to let the words flow like the sacred Ganga, carrying all impurities away while remaining eternally pure in their source.

"The artist, like Krishna's flute, must become empty to be filled with divine music."
कविता ही प्रार्थना है

The Dance of Shiva

Memory itself performs the eternal dance of Nataraja—creation, preservation, destruction, and renewal. Each recalled moment dies to be reborn, more vivid than its original incarnation. The cosmic dancer's drum beats the rhythm of remembrance.

நடராஜரின் நடனம் நினைவுகளின் லீலை

अध्यात्म और साहित्य

The great Tamil poets knew what I discovered through my literary wanderings: that the soul's geography cannot be mapped with external coordinates. The inner landscape has its own seasons, its own monsoons of memory, its own harvests of meaning.

"Every work of art is a spiritual autobiography written in symbols only the heart can decipher."

समाधि और स्मृति

The yogic state of समाधि and the Proustian moment of total recall are identical phenomena—the dissolution of time's tyranny, the merger of subject and object, the recognition that consciousness itself is the only true reality.

தியானம் மற்றும் நினைவு - ஒரே உண்மையின் இரு முகங்கள்
स्मृतिस्समाधिः

The Eternal Beloved

Albertine was never a person but a principle—the same divine feminine that the Tamil poets called தையல், that the Sanskrit mystics knew as प्रकृति. She was माया made manifest, the eternal play of consciousness with itself through the medium of love and loss.

"Love is the recognition of the Self in the apparent Other, jealousy the forgetting of this truth."

वेदान्त और Verlaine

The Upanishadic truth "तत् त्वम् असि" echoes through all great poetry. Verlaine's symbolism, like Kalidasa's imagery, points beyond the veil of appearances to the unified field of consciousness where poet, poem, and reader merge into one luminous awareness.

அந்த உண்மையே நீ - பண்டையத் தமிழரின் பாடல்

Time as Serpent

The serpent Shesha upon whom Vishnu reclines is time itself—not linear progression but eternal recurrence, the ouroboros of memory consuming and creating itself. Past and future coexist in the eternal now of artistic vision.

कालसर्प योग स्मृति

The Upanishads of Involuntary Memory

What I called "involuntary memory" the rishis knew as स्वप्न—not mere dreams but glimpses of the eternal truth that underlies the parade of phenomena. The madeleine was my श्रुति, my heard revelation, as potent as any Vedic hymn.

"Truth is not discovered but remembered; the soul knows what it has always known."
உண்மை என்பது மறந்த நினைவின் திரும்புதல்

रसावाद

The Sanskrit theory of रस explains what Western aesthetics fumbles toward—that art's purpose is not representation but the evocation of fundamental emotions that connect us to the cosmic order. Each sentence must carry its rasa like a temple carrying its deity.

शांत रस सर्वोत्तम

The Geography of the Soul

Combray was never in France but in the चक्र system of memory—the thousand-petaled lotus where all places and times converge. The temple bells of Tamil Nadu ring with the same frequency as those imagined church bells of my childhood.

ஆன்மாவின் புவியியல் - எல்லா இடங்களும் ஒன்றே

अद्वैत और Art

Shankara's non-dualism illuminates the artistic process: the observer and observed, the writer and written, reveal themselves as modifications of one consciousness. Literature becomes a form of meditation, a technology for recognizing the Self.

"The highest art erases the boundary between creator and creation, revealing the seamless fabric of being."

Sacred Jealousy

Even jealousy, that tormented passion, revealed itself as a spiritual practice when seen through Vedantic eyes. It is the ego's desperate attempt to possess what can only be experienced—like trying to capture the moon's reflection in water.

பொறாமை என்பது மாயையின் கடைசி ஆடல்
ईर्श्या माया अंतिम नृत्य

The Mandala of Narrative

Every great novel is a मंडल—a sacred circle containing infinite patterns within patterns. The structure mirrors the cosmos itself: wheels within wheels, stories within stories, all revolving around the central emptiness that contains everything.

प्राण और प्रोज़

The breath that sustains life is the same rhythm that sustains prose. Each sentence breathes in possibility and breathes out meaning. The paragraph becomes a pranayama practice, the entire work a meditation on the breath of consciousness.

சுவாசம் மற்றும் சொற்கள் - இரண்டும் வாழ்வின் தாளம்

The Bhakti of Literature

Writing, I discovered, is a form of bhakti—devotional surrender to the infinite through the finite medium of words. Each sentence becomes an offering, each paragraph a prayer, the entire work a temple erected to the unknown god of meaning.

"The writer is both priest and sacrifice in the ritual of creation."

सत्यम् शिवम् सुन्दरम्

Truth, consciousness, beauty—the ancient trinity revealed itself in every authentic literary moment. What appears as aesthetic experience is actually spiritual recognition, the soul remembering its true nature through the mirror of art.

உண்மை, நன்மை, அழகு - கலையின் மூன்று முகங்கள்
सत्यं शिवं सुन्दरं सर्वत्र

The Final Recognition

In the end, I understood that my search for lost time was the eternal human quest that the Upanishads call आत्मविचार—self-inquiry. Time is never lost because the Self exists beyond time. Memory is not retrieval but recognition of what we have always been.

"The past exists not behind us but within us, not as history but as eternity."
நேரம் இழக்கப்படவில்லை, மறைக்கப்பட்டது மட்டுமே
