what is a formulation of truth?

May be it's a bellwether the declining currency of honesty that aformulationoftruth.com still existed to register as a domain. Many folks make a lot of claims in the name of truth. I make no claim with regard to this questionnaire and its ability to discover something essential within or about us. The phrase 'a formulation of truth' I translated from the Sasnskrit word brahmā, itself the nominative singular (i.e. subject) form of brahman. Insofar as a reader of Sanskrit would be concerned, this undeclined word 'brahman' would not be found used in the language--a word is always in relation to a verb (except in the 6th voice, or genitive where a word has an adjectival relationship to another noun in the sentence). I digress as I am wont to do with such a beautiful language.

vividhā racanā: variegated composition

This website serves the Proust questionnaire dba karuppacāmi keḷvittaḷ. I chose the name karuppacāmi because it translates into ஆங்கலம் (āngalaṁ) as something like 'dark lord', and darkness appeals to me. I chose it also to reference the Tamil culture, which I stand in awe before; as well as comic relief in memorium a கழக்கஞ்ச (Kikkuñcu) I raised on handground pulses the summer of 2013.

Howsoever distinct the dark protector of boundaries from the incarnation of śiva as the horror of time, Kāla bhairava, for time consumes everything, I have cultivated a sense of self within the Sanskrit and Tamil languages now for over half of a lifetime. Being a perpetual outsider to a culture and way of life infinitely more appealing to the heartmind than this culture into which I was born and the kuladeva, or family god I know, my take is at times irreverent. What else is there to be but irreverent toward a culture in which, technically, I am a perpetual outcaste? Therefore intentional is this my curious admixture of reverence with its antonym as I seek to live by heady truths concerning the nature of reality.

The Proust questionnaire now dba forever more as the karuppacāmi keḷvittaḷ (the karuppacāmi questionnaire) from this domain has no recognized relationship to the word brahman. Incidently, a name of god. Did nnot plan that. It's just the domain I registered years ago and on which I play today. Click the link, migrate your site or start a VIrtual Private Server with flokinet.is — your patronage of a company I've found delightful to work with for over a decade now would go a long way in keeping the development of aformulationoftruth.com ongoing. Alternatively, reach out through the contact page if you wish to donate to the site's ongoing development.

Clearly as a vidyarthi of Sanskrit in the 21st c. I am not one for practicality, but beauty. And for that reason alone I create the site: reason being I think the world will become more beautiful as folks playfully engage these thirty-five personal, probing questions. The site it set up using industry standard encryption that renders your answers safely stored in a database. Anything anyone writes in the text boxes here is encrypted. Once all 35 questions are answered, there exists an option to create a profile and render your answers public--available to others who themselves also completed the questionnaire--or maintain a private profile. Private profiles are visible but no one can read the responses of a user who's elected a private profile. Just like the webhosting company I lease the domain from, I am a big proponent of privacy. I mourn the disregard of individual privacy, and the erosion of modern subjects' rights thereto in our contemporary world. Shame be upon those who would see it erode further.

Lastly, I wish to promote the questionnaire because of my own experiences responding to it. Contemporary culture moves much, much faster than the historical cultures that have attracted the heartmind of this person I operate on the daily. And I see--I have experience--being multiple persons within a single lifetime. I answered the questionnaire at age 20 and again at age 32. Looking back upon the person who answered at 20 from 32 was remarkable. Less so remarkable looking back upon the 32 year-old's answers from the vantage of today. Nonetheless, they who answered the same questions 12 years apart are one & the same person. And I think this kind of points to the shifting sands of windblown habitus, culturally speaking. We are each one of us changing and that is OK. We are able to change and we can witness others change. That is all for now.
