The bonds that unite another person
to ourselves exist only in our mind.
Memory as it grows fainter relaxes

them, and notwithstanding the
illusion by which we would fain be
cheated and with which, out of love,
friendship, politeness, deference,
duty, we cheat other people, we
exist alone. Man is the creature that
cannot emerge from himself, that
knows his fellows only in himself;
when he asserts the contrary, he is
lying
