गोप्यता privacy & transparency

This site is built on principles of respect, privacy, and openness. Below is a complete and honest accounting of our privacy practices, data handling, hosting infrastructure, and the legal frameworks that govern your data.

🇮🇸 Jurisdiction: Iceland

This site and its encrypted database are hosted in Reykjavík, Iceland by FlokiNET — a provider known for defending its clients against state overreach.

Iceland is not a member of the Five Eyes, Nine Eyes, or Fourteen Eyes intelligence-sharing alliances — the anglophone surveillance network that binds the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and their European partners (France, Denmark, the Netherlands, Norway, Germany, Belgium, Italy, Sweden, and Spain) into a single signals-intelligence apparatus. Iceland has no SIGINT-sharing agreements with any of these states. The NSA, GCHQ, the BND, the DGSE, and their partner agencies have no legal or treaty-based mechanism to compel Icelandic providers to hand over data, install backdoors, or grant silent access. American subpoenas, National Security Letters, FISA court orders, and European Intelligence and Situation Centre (EU INTCEN) requests carry zero legal force in Iceland. The same applies to requests channelled through Europol or the EU Counter-Terrorism Coordinator — Iceland’s non-EU status means these bodies have no direct authority over Icelandic hosting providers.

Iceland struck down blanket data retention laws following EU Court of Justice rulings. Providers are not obligated to retain traffic data, connection logs, or metadata. There is nothing to seize because nothing is kept. Article 71 of the Icelandic Constitution guarantees the privacy of correspondence. Icelandic courts have historically been protective of these rights — the small judiciary and strong constitutional tradition create real friction against state access, not performative obstacles. Iceland ranks #3 globally for press freedom (Reporters Without Borders, 2024) and has pioneered journalist source-protection laws that most democracies have yet to match.

Cross-border requests must go through Mutual Legal Assistance Treaties, which Iceland processes deliberately and carefully. Non-EU status adds procedural layers that intelligence agencies accustomed to Five Eyes speed find inconvenient by design.

Our posture: We minimize what we collect, encrypt what we must store, and retain no access logs. If served with a valid Icelandic court order — the only kind that matters here — we could provide only ciphertext and metadata we do not keep. We cannot surrender what we do not have.

🔐 Data Collection & Encryption

What we collect:

  • Email addresses (Newsletter): Encrypted using AES-256-GCM with PBKDF2 key derivation (100,000 iterations) before storage.
  • Questionnaire responses: Encrypted with age (X25519) before storage. We cannot read your answers.
  • Authentication: Magic links via email. We do not store passwords.
  • IP addresses: Logged only for newsletter signup rate-limiting (anti-spam). Not linked to questionnaire responses. Not retained long-term.

📜 Your Rights

Under GDPR (applicable in Iceland via the EEA) and general principle:

  • Right to access: Request a copy of your data at any time
  • Right to deletion: Request complete removal of your account and all associated data.

    In many jurisdictions, “deleted” is a polite fiction. Data retention laws compel providers to keep copies of your data for months or years after you ask for it to be removed — silently, in backups, in logs, in systems designed to comply with law enforcement before they comply with you. In the United States, providers may retain data indefinitely under litigation holds, national security letters, or simply because their terms of service permit it. In EU member states, mandatory data retention directives can override your deletion request.

    Iceland has no blanket data retention law. The Icelandic Parliament struck down such legislation following EU Court of Justice rulings that found mass retention incompatible with fundamental rights. Icelandic providers are under no obligation to retain traffic data, connection metadata, or user records after the service relationship ends. When we delete your data, there is no law requiring us to keep a shadow copy. There is no backup retention mandate. There is no quiet exception for intelligence agencies.

    On this site, deletion means: your HMAC-hashed email identifier is removed from the database, your encrypted questionnaire responses are purged, your session records are destroyed, and any associated rate-limit entries are cleared. We run no off-site backups that could resurrect what you asked us to destroy. Deleted means deleted — not archived, not retained for compliance, not preserved in case someone with a badge asks for it later.

  • Right to portability: Export your questionnaire responses
  • Newsletter unsubscribe: Instant removal via the unsubscribe link (no questions asked)

To exercise these rights, email us.
